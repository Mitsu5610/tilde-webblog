Welcome to the City of Pines

The sixth and final day of our DJM Educational Tour brought us to Baguio City — the summer capital of the Philippines, nestled in the Cordillera Mountains at 1,500 m above sea level. The pine-scented cool air, colonial landmarks, military pride, and mountain panoramas made Day 6 one of the most memorable days of the entire tour.

⛪
01
First Stop

Bell Church

Our Lady of Atonement Cathedral — Kisad Road, Baguio City

The Bell Church, formally the Our Lady of Atonement Cathedral, is one of Baguio's most distinctive and photographed landmarks. Its vibrant pagoda-style architecture, colorful tile murals, and decorative statues blend Chinese and Catholic traditions into a uniquely Filipino expression of faith. Strolling through the grounds, we were struck by the intricate detail of every tile and the peaceful atmosphere despite being a popular tourist destination.

  • ⛪ Pagoda-style architecture blending Chinese & Catholic traditions
  • 🎨 Vibrant tile murals and intricately crafted statues
  • 🙏 One of Baguio's most iconic spiritual landmarks

02
Second Stop

The Mansion

Official Presidential Summer Residence — Leonard Wood Road, Baguio

The Mansion is the official summer residence of the President of the Philippines. Built during the American colonial era, it boasts Victorian-style architecture, iconic ornate wrought-iron gates, and immaculate formal gardens. Even from outside the main gate, the grandeur of the facade was breathtaking. The gates themselves are among the most photographed structures in Baguio — and we made sure to get our group photo there!

  • 🏛 Victorian colonial architecture from the American era
  • 🚪 Famous wrought-iron presidential gates
  • 🌸 Beautifully manicured formal gardens and grounds

Third Stop

Philippine Military Academy

PMA — Fort del Pilar, Loakan Road, Baguio City

The Philippine Military Academy (PMA), known as the "West Point of Asia," is the nation's premier military educational institution. Set in the sprawling Fort del Pilar campus, PMA trains the future officers of the Armed Forces of the Philippines. We were awed by the vast Borromeo Field parade grounds, the imposing barracks, and the campus museum. The sense of discipline, honor, and service that permeates the entire academy left a profound impression on everyone.

  • 🎖 "West Point of Asia" — premier Philippine military school
  • 🏗 Grand Borromeo Field parade grounds
  • 🇵🇭 Museum showcasing Philippine military heritage

04
Final Stop

Mines View Park

Outlook Drive, Baguio City — Panoramic Cordillera Vista

Mines View Park was the perfect final stop for Day 6 — a breathtaking overlook above the old Benguet mining town offering sweeping panoramic views of the Cordillera mountain ranges. The cool mountain air, the vibrant local market selling fresh strawberry products, Igorot crafts, and pasalubong, and the opportunity to wear traditional Cordillera costumes for souvenir photos made this a wonderfully lively and colorful send-off to our Baguio adventure.

  • 🏔 Sweeping panoramic views of the Cordillera ranges
  • 🍓 Fresh strawberries, local crafts & pasalubong market
  • 👔 Traditional Igorot costume souvenir photo opportunities
